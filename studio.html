02 — Studio

Design that feels intentional

If it doesn't look right, it doesn't work. And you always know when it doesn't look right

Shelly — Design & Creative Direction

Everyone can tell when something is designed well. Almost nobody can tell you why. That gap is where the Studio lives: the typography choices, the pacing of an edit, the two millimetres of spacing that separate premium from almost.

The work here isn't decoration. It's direction. Before anything gets made, there's a visual decision about what it should feel like. A film, an identity, a deck, a feed: same rule. Get that decision right and everything downstream gets easier. Get it wrong and no amount of polish saves it.

The work

01

Brand films from the wild

Secluded Africa

Films and social content shot in Kenya for a safari brand that refuses to look like a safari brochure. The brief was atmosphere over itinerary: what it feels like to be there, not what's included. Cinematic storytelling in the most literal sense.

02

A content system that never sleeps

Liberty International Tourism Group

Five hundred plus designed posts a year, across global markets, for over three years, without the feed ever looking like a template. Building a visual language strong enough to stay consistent and flexible enough to stay alive is the hardest kind of design work there is. It just doesn't look like it.

03

Decks that win rooms

Royal Enfield & others

Pitch and presentation design treated as a craft of its own, including the winning event pitch for the Royal Enfield Classic 350 launch. A deck has one job: make the room lean in before slide five. Everything about how we design them serves that.

Every project here starts the same way: a moodboard before a mockup, a direction before a deliverable. Not because process is sacred, but because taste needs a reference point, something to hold the work accountable to.

The Studio runs on that discipline. Video, identity, social, decks: different formats, same standard. If it doesn't look intentional, it goes back.

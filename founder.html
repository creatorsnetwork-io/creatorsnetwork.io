The Founder

Movement, stories, play

I'm someone who needs movement, stories, and play to stay alive

Himanshu Arora — Founder & CEO

Creators Network didn't start with a business plan. It started with a call that said, let's try. An idea held together by Google Docs, hustle, and belief. No investors, no safety nets, a borrowed laptop, and a pandemic outside the window.

Six years later it's still run the same way: one founder, no board, boutique by choice. Built to do good work, not to do more of it.

Before the network, there were rooms. Ten years of them: stadiums, ceremonies, arenas, stages. The kind of events where a hundred thousand people show up and nothing is allowed to go wrong. That decade is still the spine of how CN thinks: begin with the story, not the setup.

The call

Started during COVID with no team and no roadmap. A borrowed laptop, a pandemic outside the window, and the first clients arriving before the company felt real. Sometimes the conditions for starting something are never right — so you start anyway.

2022
2022

Taking charge

CN turned international. The agency's centre of gravity shifted from Indian market noise to cross-border B2B work — Middle East, Europe, beyond. This was the year it stopped feeling like a startup and started feeling like a company.

2025
2025

Testing new waters

The UAE entity was registered. Dubai stopped being a plan and became a commitment. CN began exploring the GCC market — quieter, more deliberate, the kind of groundwork that doesn't announce itself before it's ready.

2026
2026

All the way in

From testing the waters to full commitment. CN moved its base to Dubai, set its sights on the GCC and beyond, and began building the kind of client relationships the region deserves — strategic, long-term, built on trust rather than transactions.
